Gary Disco

1901-1946

Gary Disco was born in 1901 in Edina, New York. From an early age, he displayed a remarkable sense of discipline and a strong work ethic, qualities that defined his life and career. When World War II broke out, Gary enlisted in the military, gaining respect for his dedication and exceptional performance.

After the war, Gary returned to Edina and noticed a lack of diners and social venues. He decided to open Gary Disco's Diner, a unique establishment. From 7 AM to 7 PM, it operated as a classic diner with staff in standard uniforms serving delicious meals. At 7 PM, the atmosphere transformed: the lights dimmed, and the diner became a disco. Staff swapped their uniforms for roller skates and military-esque outfits, adding a dynamic flair to the nightly festivities.

Tragically, Gary Disco passed away in 1946, but his legacy lived on. The diner was taken over by three Canadian owners who upheld Gary's traditions. They continued enforcing the double outfit rule, ensuring the diner remained a cherished landmark in Edina, known for its excellent food and vibrant nightlife.

During the war, Gary was often seen doodling the logo for his dream diner on scraps of paper. Today, we celebrate his vision by still using his original logo and font.
